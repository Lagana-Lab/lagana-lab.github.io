Mujahid (Ali)'s work lies at the intersection of AI, biomedical informatics, and clinical genomics—driving innovations that transform unstructured scientific literature and patient data into actionable insights.

He architected OncoCITE, an AI-powered framework for precision oncology, combining RAG pipelines, ReAct agents, and large language models to surface clinically relevant insights from genomic data. He has also investigated the use of machine learning for monitoring CAR-T therapy- related toxicities and is leading the development of a multimodal patient platform integrating EHRs, lab data, and advanced foundation models like Geneformer and Mistral.

Previously a TA and graduate researcher at NYU, he is passionate about building tools that make complex science more accessible—for researchers, clinicians, and patients alike.
